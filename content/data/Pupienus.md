<p><b>Pupienus</b> (<a class="mw-redirect" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin_language" title="Latin language">Latin</a>: <i lang="la">Marcus Clodius Pupienus Maximus Augustus</i>; born c. 165/170<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> â€“ 29 July 238), also known as <b>Pupienus Maximus</b>, was <a class="mw-redirect" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Emperor" title="Roman Emperor">Roman Emperor</a> with <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balbinus" title="Balbinus">Balbinus</a> for three months in 238, during the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Year_of_the_Six_Emperors" title="Year of the Six Emperors">Year of the Six Emperors</a>. In most contemporary texts Pupienus is referred to by his <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognomen" title="Cognomen">cognomen</a> "Maximus" rather than by his second <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_naming_conventions" title="Roman naming conventions">nomen</a> (family name) Pupienus.
