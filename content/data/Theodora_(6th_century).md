<p><b>Theodora</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt"><a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˌ/: secondary stress follows">ˌ</span><span title="/θ/: 'th' in 'thigh'">θ</span><span title="/iː/: 'ee' in 'fleece'">iː</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="'d' in 'dye'">d</span><span title="/ɔːr/: 'ar' in 'war'">ɔːr</span><span title="/ə/: 'a' in 'about'">ə</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_language" title="Greek language">Greek</a>: Θεοδώρα; c. 500 – 28 June 548) was <a class="mw-redirect" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empress" title="Empress">empress</a> of the <a class="mw-redirect" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Roman_Empire" title="Eastern Roman Empire">Eastern Roman Empire</a> by marriage to Emperor <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Justinian_I" title="Justinian I">Justinian I</a>. She was one of the most influential and powerful of the Eastern Roman empresses, albeit from a humble background.<sup class="reference" id="cite_ref-1"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> Along with her spouse, she is a saint in the <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox_Church" title="Eastern Orthodox Church">Eastern Orthodox Church</a>, commemorated on November 14.
